Heavy Metals in Water: Risks & Solutions

Understanding the impact of heavy metal contamination is the first step towards ensuring safe drinking water. Below are details on common contaminants.

Arsenic (As)

Health Effects

Long-term exposure to arsenic from drinking water can cause cancer (skin, bladder, lung), skin lesions, and has been associated with cardiovascular disease and diabetes.

Mitigation Strategies

Reverse osmosis, activated alumina filters, and ion exchange systems are effective at removing arsenic from water at the household or community level.

Lead (Pb)

Health Effects

Lead is highly toxic, especially to children. It can cause severe damage to the brain and nervous system, leading to learning disabilities, behavioral problems, and lowered IQ.

Mitigation Strategies

Replacing old lead pipes and plumbing fixtures is the most permanent solution. Point-of-use water filters certified for lead removal are also effective.

Cadmium (Cd)

Health Effects

Cadmium exposure can lead to kidney damage, fragile bones (Itai-Itai disease), and is classified as a human carcinogen.

Mitigation Strategies

Effective removal methods include reverse osmosis, ion exchange, and lime softening. Preventing industrial discharge is key to source control.

Mercury (Hg)

Health Effects

Mercury is a potent neurotoxin. Exposure can cause tremors, memory loss, and damage to the kidneys and central nervous system. It is particularly harmful to fetal development.

Mitigation Strategies

Granular activated carbon (GAC) filters and reverse osmosis can remove mercury. Reducing emissions from coal-fired power plants and mining is crucial for prevention.
