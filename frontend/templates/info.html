Understanding Heavy Metals

Learn about common heavy metals, their sources, and their impact on health and the environment.

Arsenic (As)

Health Impacts

Long-term exposure can lead to skin lesions, cancer, and developmental effects. It primarily affects the skin, nervous system, and respiratory system.

Common Sources

Natural deposits, industrial waste, agricultural pesticides, and contaminated water.

Cadmium (Cd)

Health Impacts

Can cause kidney damage, bone problems, and is a known carcinogen. Food and cigarette smoke are major sources of exposure.

Common Sources

Industrial processes, batteries, pigments, and phosphate fertilizers.

Lead (Pb)

Health Impacts

Highly toxic, especially to children. It can cause neurological damage, developmental delays, and kidney problems.

Common Sources

Old pipes, paints, industrial emissions, and batteries.

Mercury (Hg)

Health Impacts

Affects the nervous, digestive, and immune systems. Can be fatal.

Common Sources

Mining, coal-burning power plants, and some industrial processes.

Chromium (Cr)

Health Impacts

Hexavalent chromium (Cr-VI) is carcinogenic and can cause respiratory problems.

Common Sources

Industrial processes like chrome plating, tanning, and pigment production.
